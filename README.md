# Workforce Analytics Dashboard

![Dashboard Image](https://github.com/Josh-Osas/HR-Workforce-Analytics-in-PowerBI/blob/main/HR%20Dashboard.png)

This project analyzes workforce data using four datasets:

## Tools Used
- **Excel**: For data cleaning, preparation, and basic analysis.
- **Power BI**: For creating dynamic, interactive dashboards and visualizations to convey insights clearly.


  This project analyzes workforce data using four datasets:
- **employee_data**: Contains employee demographics and employment details.
- **employment_survey_data**: Includes employee satisfaction, engagement, and work-life balance survey results.
- **recruitment_data**: Tracks recruitment information such as new hires and department distribution.
- **training_and_development_data**: Details training costs and employee development participation.
  

## Project Overview

This dashboard was built using **Power BI** to provide actionable insights into the workforce and HR performance.


### Key Insights and Takeaways

- **Employee Demographics**:
  - The workforce is diverse, with a healthy distribution across **race** (Asian, Black, White) and **gender** (56.07% female). This indicates potential strength in diverse perspectives and approaches within teams.
  - A **significant portion of employees** (1,259) are aged **60 and above**, suggesting the need for succession planning, knowledge transfer, and potential retirement-related adjustments in staffing.


- **Age Group Distribution**:
  - The **age group 30-39** is the second-largest, showing a **strong core of mid-career employees**. This is an ideal group to focus on for leadership development and retention strategies.
  - The **under-30 group (287 employees)** is smaller, which may indicate opportunities to **attract and retain younger talent** by offering growth opportunities, mentorship, and development programs.


- **Employee Engagement**:
  - **Engagement score (2.94)** is **slightly below the ideal** and indicates room for improvement in employee engagement and involvement. Initiatives such as **employee recognition programs**, **team-building activities**, and **career development opportunities** could increase overall satisfaction.
  - **Job Satisfaction (3.02)** is relatively decent but still leaves room for improvement. Focus on identifying key pain points via **employee surveys** and enhancing work conditions could raise this score.


- **Performance and Productivity**:
  - The **Performance Score (2.15)** signals that employees might not be fully utilizing their potential. **Targeted training** and **performance management** could help boost individual and team productivity.
  - **Monthly Productivity**: There is a noticeable peak in **March** (589 employees) and **October** (580 employees), with a low in **February** (489 employees). This seasonal variation could be a point of focus for **optimizing workloads** and adjusting strategies during slow months to maintain productivity.


- **Training and Development**:
  - The **total training cost** of **$1,670,942** emphasizes a significant investment in employee development. Ensuring that training programs are **aligned with organizational goals** will help in maximizing the ROI on training initiatives and better equip employees for future roles.
